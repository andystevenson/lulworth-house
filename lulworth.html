Lulworth Village

Some of Dorset’s most visited landmarks are found in Lulworth and it is also part of the Jurassic Coast World Heritage Site..

Lulworth Castle

The castle is an early 17th-century Elizabethan hunting lodge erected in the style of a revival fortified castle. The parks and surrounding grounds are stunning.

The Castle Inn

A stones throw from the house. It is an excellent gastro pub.

Lulworth Cove Inn

A must visit following a coastal walk or fishing trip.

Relax by the beach

There are plenty of seaside cafés to enjoy.

Durdle Door

Iconic rock formation punctuates the splendour of the cove.

Sunset Beach Walks

The cove's landscape is amazing as the sun goes down.

Fishing Discovery

Seafarers paradise as many boats/yachts anchor in the cove.

Coastal Walks

Popular at any time of year. Lulworth Cove is a world reknowned highlight of the Jurassic Coast.

The Cove

Spectacular at any time, but the peace and tranquility of early mornings are hard to beat.
